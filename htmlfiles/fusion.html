Incorporation

The Company was originally incorporated as Ambience Fincap Private Limited on September 5, 1994, in New Delhi, India as a private limited company under the Companies Act, 1956, as amended. The company was registered as a non-deposit accepting non-banking financial company (“NBFC”) under Section 45IA of the Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 and RBI granted a certificate of registration bearing registration no. B-14.02857. Subsequently, the name of the Company was changed to ‘Fusion Micro Finance Private Limited.’ The company was granted NBFC – Microfinance Institution (“NBFC-MFI”) status by the RBI with effect from January 28, 2014, and a modified certificate of registration bearing registration no. B-14.02857 was issued by the RBI to this effect. further name of the company was changed to Fusion Micro Finance Limited upon conversion to a public limited company.

Business of the Company

  • It is engaged in providing financial services to unserved and underserved women in rural and peri-rural areas across India.
  • It is one of the youngest companies among the top NBFC-MFIs in India in terms of AUM as of June 30, 2022.
  • Its business runs on a joint liability group-lending model, wherein a small number of women form a group (typically comprising five to seven members) and guarantee one another’s loans.

Companies Offerings:

Microfinance: Microfinance, also called microcredit, is the provision of small credit to low-income individuals or groups who otherwise would have no other access to financial services Products under microfinance are as under:

  1. Income-generating loans: Income-generating loans are its core loan product for use by women in rural areas and are intended to provide capital for their small businesses.
  2. Top-up Loans: It is granted to existing customers to manage interim working capital requirements for their existing business.
  3. Emergency Loans: It is granted to existing customers to fund urgent financial requirements arising out of unforeseen events such as health emergencies, natural disasters, and family bereavements.
  4. Cross-sale Loans: Leverage its existing customer base to provide cross-sell loans that are utilized for livelihood and productivity-enhancing purposes.

MSME: It is also providing MSME loans to eligible enterprises, and here eligible enterprises must be a manufacturer, traders, or service providers, and have an investment limit of less than ₹1,500,000, the Products under MSME are as under:

  1. Unsecured business loan
  2. Secured business loan
  3. Machinery loan
